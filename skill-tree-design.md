# Skill Tree Design

## Design Philosophy

SkillKingdom uses broad base skills that grow through repeated use. As a character improves, those broad skills unlock more focused skill branches.

The goal is to support many character identities without forcing the player into a single class path too early.

Examples:

- A gatherer can become a miner, forester, herbalist, fisher, or hunter.
- A crafter can specialize in smithing, alchemy, enchanting, cooking, or equipment production.
- A fighter can grow into weapon, armor, shield, or unarmed specialties.
- A mage can progress from basic magic into Fundamental, Esoteric, and Primal magical forces.
- A survival-focused character can track, sneak, spot danger, and navigate the world.
- A social character can trade, persuade, intimidate, and influence others.
- A scholar can develop knowledge that unlocks discoveries, weaknesses, recipes, locations, and hidden mechanics.

Skills should feel like things the character becomes better at through action.

---

## Core Skill Rules

A skill can:

- Gain XP from actions, combat, crafting, exploration, study, conversation, or discovery.
- Unlock child skills at certain levels.
- Improve success chance, action speed, resource yield, combat performance, crafting quality, or discovery chance.
- Unlock recipes, techniques, spells, locations, titles, classes, or special actions.
- Become visible once discovered and remain visible afterward.

Skill XP should usually be awarded even on failure, but successful actions should award more.

---

## Base Skills

These are the broadest skill families.

| Skill | Role |
|---|---|
| Gathering | Harvesting raw materials from the world. |
| Crafting | Creating, refining, repairing, and improving items. |
| Martial | Physical combat, weapons, armor, shields, and battlefield instincts. |
| Magic | Mana use, spell control, magical study, and supernatural power. |
| Survival | Wilderness movement, scouting, stealth, tracking, and danger awareness. |
| Social | Trade, persuasion, intimidation, and dealing with people. |
| Lore | Knowledge, study, identification, history, monsters, ruins, and magical theory. |

---

## Suggested Unlock Pattern

Default unlock pattern:

| Skill Level | Unlock Type |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Skill is known but weak, if already discovered. |
| 5 | Minor passive bonus or basic technique. |
| 10 | Unlock child skills or basic specialization. |
| 25 | Unlock improved actions, higher material tiers, or advanced techniques. |
| 50 | Unlock expert specializations, rare materials, or class paths. |
| 100 | Unlock mastery paths, legendary materials, or major class evolution. |

This pattern can be adjusted per skill.

# Gathering Skill Tree

## Gathering

Gathering represents general experience collecting useful materials from the world.

**Unlocks at Gathering 10:**

| Skill | Description |
|---|---|
| Woodcutting | Gathering logs, branches, bark, and forest materials. |
| Mining | Gathering stone, ore, gems, and underground materials. |
| Foraging | Gathering herbs, mushrooms, fruit, fibers, and wild ingredients. |
| Fishing | Catching fish and aquatic resources. |
| Skinning | Harvesting hides, pelts, scales, bones, and usable creature parts. |

---

## Gathering Progression Philosophy

Gathering branches should primarily unlock:

- Tougher materials.
- Rarer materials.
- Better yield from easier materials.
- Lower stamina cost for familiar materials.
- Better chance to discover unusual resources.
- Reduced failure or injury chance.

Example progression:

| Skill Level | Example Unlock |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Gather common materials poorly. |
| 10 | Reliably gather common materials. |
| 25 | Gather sturdy or uncommon materials. |
| 50 | Gather rare materials. |
| 75 | Gather dangerous or magical materials. |
| 100 | Gather legendary materials. |

# Magic Skill Tree

## Magic

Magic represents general magical proficiency, mana sensitivity, mana handling, spell stability, and resistance to magical backlash.

Magic does not immediately mean the character can cast every type of spell. Instead, the base Magic skill unlocks access to deeper tiers of magical study.

**Magic unlocks:**

| Magic Level | Unlock |
|---:|---|
| 10 | Tier 1: Fundamental Magic |
| 50 | Tier 2: Esoteric Magic |
| 100 | Tier 3: Primal Magic |

---

## Tier 1: Fundamental Magic

Fundamental Magic includes magical forces most closely tied to the natural world, living existence, and common metaphysical opposites.

Unlocked when Magic reaches level 10.

| Skill | Description |
|---|---|
| Fire | Heat, flame, combustion, destruction, and cleansing. |
| Water | Water, ice, mist, flow, healing support, and adaptability. |
| Earth | Stone, soil, metal, stability, defense, and shaping. |
| Air | Wind, breath, sound, pressure, speed, and movement. |
| Life | Healing, growth, vitality, purification, and restoration. |
| Death | Decay, entropy, draining, curses, spirits, and endings. |
| Light | Radiance, revelation, protection, clarity, and truth. |
| Dark | Shadow, concealment, fear, silence, secrecy, and hidden things. |

---

## Tier 2: Esoteric Magic

Esoteric Magic includes forces that are harder to perceive, understand, or control. These schools often require deeper magical theory, unusual perception, stronger will, or dangerous experimentation.

Unlocked when Magic reaches level 50.

| Skill | Description |
|---|---|
| Astral | Stars, dreams, divination, celestial influence, distant perception, and navigation beyond the physical. |
| Spirit | Souls, ghosts, possession, exorcism, ancestral power, and communication with the dead or unseen. |
| Psionic | Mind, emotion, memory, illusion, telepathy, mental force, and perception manipulation. |
| Blood | Sacrifice, vitality, lineage, binding, body manipulation, and power drawn through living essence. |
| Arcane | Pure mana, spell structure, wards, metamagic, force, spellcraft, and magical refinement. |

---

## Tier 3: Primal Magic

Primal Magic includes reality-shaping forces. These are rare, dangerous, difficult to control, and likely tied to late-game progression.

Unlocked when Magic reaches level 100.

| Skill | Description |
|---|---|
| Void | Nothingness, absence, silence, anti-magic, erasure, and emptiness. |
| Time | Acceleration, delay, aging, prediction, loops, and temporal distortion. |
| Space | Distance, gravity, portals, storage, folding, and displacement. |
| Chaos | Mutation, probability, instability, wild magic, transformation, and reality disruption. |

---

## Magic Progression Notes

Tier 1 magic should be useful but understandable. A character can burn, freeze, heal, shield, conceal, illuminate, decay, or shape common forces.

Tier 2 magic should feel stranger and more specialized. It should involve souls, minds, stars, dreams, blood, and unseen influences.

Tier 3 magic should feel dangerous and reality-altering. Early use should be limited, unstable, expensive, or risky.

Suggested limitation for Tier 3:

| Tier 3 Skill | Early Use Example |
|---|---|
| Void | Silence a small magical effect or weaken a spell. |
| Time | Slightly hasten or delay an action. |
| Space | Improve storage, reach, or short displacement. |
| Chaos | Introduce controlled randomness or mutation at a cost. |

# Class Discovery Notes

Classes should sit on top of skills rather than replacing them.

Suggested model:

```text
Actions -> Skill XP -> Skill Unlocks -> Pattern Recognition -> Class Options
```

A class can unlock when the player meets a pattern of skill levels, actions, discoveries, or achievements.

Example:

| Class | Possible Requirements |
|---|---|
| Scout | Survival 10, Tracking 5, Stealth 5, Awareness 5 |
| Forester | Gathering 10, Woodcutting 10, Survival 5 |
| Miner | Gathering 10, Mining 10 |
| Duelist | Martial 15, One Hand 10, Light Armor 5 |
| Shieldbearer | Martial 15, Shields 10, Heavy Armor 5 |
| Apprentice Mage | Magic 10, any Fundamental Magic skill 5 |
| Herbalist | Gathering 10, Foraging 10, Material Lore 5 |
| Apothecary | Alchemy 10, Foraging 5, Material Lore 5 |
| Merchant | Social 10, Trade 10 |
| Scholar | Lore 15, any Lore branch 10 |

Classes may grant titles, small bonuses, special actions, or unlock paths. They should not erase the player's existing skills.
